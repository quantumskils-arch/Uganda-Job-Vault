If you've been applying for jobs in Uganda and hearing nothing back, the problem might not be your qualifications — it might be that a computer never let your CV through.

## What Is ATS?

ATS (Applicant Tracking System) is software that employers use to manage the flood of applications they receive for every opening. When you submit your CV online, the ATS scans it for:

- **Keywords** matching the job description
- **Formatting** that the system can parse
- **Required fields** like education dates, job titles, and contact info

If your CV fails any of these checks, it gets filtered out automatically. No human ever sees it.

## The 5 Rules for ATS-Friendly CVs

### 1. Use Standard Section Headings
The ATS looks for standard section headers to categorize your information. Use exactly:

| Do use | Don't use |
|---|---|
| Work Experience | My Career Journey |
| Education | Academic Background |
| Skills | What I Can Do |
| Professional Summary | About Me |

### 2. No Tables or Columns
Many ATS systems can't read content inside tables or multi-column layouts. If your CV uses columns (common in creative templates), the ATS will read the text in the wrong order or miss it entirely.

**Single column, top-to-bottom.** That's what machines read best.

### 3. Match Keywords from the Job Description
This is the most important rule. Read the job description carefully and identify 10-15 key phrases — skills, tools, certifications, soft skills. Then make sure those exact phrases appear naturally in your CV.

For example, if the job asks for "grant proposal writing," don't write "wrote proposals for funding." Write exactly "grant proposal writing."

### 4. Save as .docx, Not PDF
In Uganda, many government and NGO HR systems still prefer `.docx` format. PDFs sometimes lose formatting or can't be parsed by older ATS systems. Check each application's requirements — but when in doubt, `.docx` is safer.

### 5. Keep It Simple
No graphics, no icons, no logos, no fancy fonts. Use a standard font like Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman at 10-12pt. Bold and italic are fine. Underlining is not.

## A Simple ATS-Ready Template

Here's a structure that works:

```
[Your Name]
[Phone] | [Email] | [LinkedIn URL]

Professional Summary
2-3 sentences. Most recent role, top skills, what you're looking for.

Work Experience
[Job Title] | [Employer] | [Start Date] - [End Date]
- Bullet point achievements
- Use numbers where possible
- Include keywords from your target role

Education
[Degree] | [Institution] | [Year]

Skills
- Technical skills, software, languages
- Soft skills backed by examples

Certifications
[Cert Name] | [Issuing Body] | [Year]
```

## Real Example

**Before (gets rejected by ATS):**
> "I am a highly motivated professional with extensive experience in the NGO sector. I have worked on various projects related to community development and capacity building."

**After (passes ATS screening):**
> "Program Manager with 6+ years of experience in NGO project management, community development, and grant compliance. Managed 4 donor-funded projects totaling UGX 2.5 billion with 95% budget utilization rate."
