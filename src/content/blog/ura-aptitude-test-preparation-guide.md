The Uganda Revenue Authority (URA) aptitude test is widely regarded as one of the most competitive entry assessments in Uganda's public service. Thousands apply. Only a handful make it through.

## What Is the URA Aptitude Test?

The URA aptitude test is a written assessment administered to shortlisted candidates for entry-level positions at URA. It tests:

- **Numerical reasoning** — Basic math, data interpretation
- **Verbal reasoning** — Comprehension, grammar, logical reasoning
- **General knowledge** — Uganda's tax system, economy, current affairs
- **Situational judgment** — How you'd handle work scenarios

## Test Format

| Section | Questions | Time | What's Tested |

## Sample Questions

### Numerical Reasoning

**Question:** A taxpayer's annual income is UGX 36,000,000. Using the 2026/27 PAYE bands (0% on first UGX 335,000/month, 10% on 335,001–500,000, 20% on 500,001–2,000,000, 25% on 2,000,001–10,000,000, 30% above 10,000,000), what is their monthly PAYE?

*Step 1: Monthly income = 36M ÷ 12 = UGX 3,000,000*
*Step 2: Tax calculation:*
- *0% on first 335,000 = 0*
- *10% on next 165,000 = 16,500*
- *20% on next 1,500,000 = 300,000*
- *25% on next 1,000,000 = 250,000*
- *Total monthly PAYE = UGX 566,500*

### Verbal Reasoning

**Question:** "URA's mandate is to assess, collect, and account for tax revenues in Uganda. The authority also facilitates trade and enforces compliance." Which of the following is NOT part of URA's mandate?

A) Assessing tax revenues
B) Collecting tax revenues
C) Setting tax rates
D) Facilitating trade

**Answer:** C — Tax rates are set by Parliament through the Ministry of Finance, not by URA.

### General Knowledge

**Question:** What is the current standard VAT rate in Uganda?

**Answer:** 18%

### Situational Judgment

**Question:** A taxpayer offers you a small gift after you help them resolve a filing issue. What do you do?

**Best answer:** Politely decline and explain that URA staff are not permitted to accept gifts from taxpayers, as per the URA Code of Conduct.

## How to Prepare

### 1. Master the Math
Numerical reasoning is where most candidates lose points. Focus on:
- Percentage calculations (especially for tax bands)
- Ratio and proportion
- Data interpretation from tables
- Basic algebra

### 2. Know Your Tax Basics
URA tests understanding of Uganda's tax system. Key topics:
- PAYE bands and calculations
- VAT rate (18%) and who must register
- Income tax for individuals vs companies
- Key URA programs and recent reforms

### 3. Stay Current
The general knowledge section tests awareness of:
- Uganda's economic performance (GDP growth, inflation)
- Recent tax policy changes (like the 2026/27 Income Tax Amendment)
- Major URA initiatives (e-filing, compliance campaigns)
- Current affairs in East Africa

### 4. Practice Timed Tests
The test is fast-paced — you have roughly 1 minute per question. Practice with a timer.

## On Test Day

- Arrive by 7:30 AM (registration starts early)
- Bring: National ID, printout of your invitation, 2 pens, a simple calculator (not phone)
- The test is paper-based in most cases
- Results are typically released within 2 weeks

## What Happens After

If you pass the aptitude test, you proceed to:
1. **Written exercise** — Usually a report or case study
2. **Panel interview** — 5-7 panel members
3. **Background checks** — Academic documents, previous employers
4. **Medical examination** — At a designated government hospital
