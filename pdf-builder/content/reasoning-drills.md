# How to Use This Pack

Almost every aptitude test in Uganda — PSC, URA, Bank of Uganda, Stanbic, DFCU, NSSF, and most government and parastatal recruitment — tests the same four reasoning skills: **numerical, verbal, logical, and situational judgement.** Master these four, and you are ready for almost any test you sit.

This pack has **90 real-style practice questions** with a **full worked answer for every single one** — not just the letter, but *why*. The explanation is where the learning happens. Do not skip it, even on questions you get right.

::: tip The smart way to drill
Do one section at a time, under a loose timer (about 60–75 seconds per question). Mark your answer before you look at the solution. When you get one wrong, read the worked answer twice and write down the *method*, not just the right letter. Method is what transfers to the real test.
:::

Answers for each section are grouped at the **end of that section**, so you can test yourself first without seeing the solution by accident.

# Section 1 — Numerical Reasoning (25 Questions)

Numerical reasoning tests how well you work with numbers, percentages, ratios, rates, and data. You do not need advanced maths — just accuracy and speed with the basics. A calculator is sometimes allowed; practise both ways.

**Q1.** A worker earns UGX 480,000 per month. After a 15% pay rise, what is the new monthly salary?

**Q2.** A shop buys an item for UGX 8,000 and sells it for UGX 10,000. What is the percentage profit?

**Q3.** A car travels 240 km in 3 hours. At the same speed, how far will it travel in 5 hours?

**Q4.** If 3 workers build a wall in 12 days, how long will 4 workers take, working at the same rate?

**Q5.** A phone costs UGX 600,000. In a sale it is reduced by 20%. What is the sale price?

**Q6.** The ratio of men to women in an office is 5:3. If there are 40 people, how many are women?

**Q7.** A tank holds 1,200 litres. It is 35% full. How many litres are in the tank?

**Q8.** A loan of UGX 2,000,000 attracts simple interest of 10% per year. How much interest is owed after 2 years?

**Q9.** A meeting starts at 9:45 am and lasts 1 hour 50 minutes. What time does it end?

**Q10.** 5 pens cost UGX 7,500. How much do 8 pens cost at the same price?

**Q11.** A class of 50 students has 60% girls. How many boys are there?

**Q12.** A trader's sales were: Mon 120,000; Tue 95,000; Wed 110,000; Thu 135,000; Fri 90,000. What were the average daily sales?

**Q13.** If a product's price rises from UGX 25,000 to UGX 30,000, what is the percentage increase?

**Q14.** A water bill is UGX 48,000 for 30 days. What is the average cost per day (to the nearest shilling)?

**Q15.** A rectangle is 12 m long and 8 m wide. What is its area?

**Q16.** A man spends 1/4 of his salary on rent and 1/3 on food. What fraction is left?

**Q17.** A train leaves at 14:20 and arrives at 17:05. How long is the journey?

**Q18.** 40% of a number is 200. What is the number?

**Q19.** A bag has 6 red, 4 blue, and 2 green balls. What is the probability of drawing a blue ball?

**Q20.** A salary of UGX 1,500,000 is taxed at 30%. What is the take-home pay?

**Q21.** A project is 3/5 complete. If 60 tasks are done, how many tasks are there in total?

**Q22.** Sugar costs UGX 4,500 per kg. How much do 2.5 kg cost?

**Q23.** A population grows from 8,000 to 9,200 in a year. What is the percentage growth?

**Q24.** A worker is paid UGX 12,000 per hour and works 7.5 hours. What is the day's pay?

**Q25.** If exchange rate is UGX 3,800 to 1 USD, how many shillings is USD 250?

<!-- pagebreak -->

## Section 1 — Worked Answers

**A1.** 15% of 480,000 = 72,000. New salary = 480,000 + 72,000 = **UGX 552,000.**

**A2.** Profit = 10,000 − 8,000 = 2,000. Percentage = (2,000 ÷ 8,000) × 100 = **25%.**

**A3.** Speed = 240 ÷ 3 = 80 km/h. In 5 hours = 80 × 5 = **400 km.**

**A4.** Total work = 3 × 12 = 36 worker-days. With 4 workers: 36 ÷ 4 = **9 days.**

**A5.** 20% of 600,000 = 120,000. Sale price = 600,000 − 120,000 = **UGX 480,000.**

**A6.** Total ratio parts = 5 + 3 = 8. Women = (3 ÷ 8) × 40 = **15 women.**

**A7.** 35% of 1,200 = 0.35 × 1,200 = **420 litres.**

**A8.** Interest per year = 10% of 2,000,000 = 200,000. Over 2 years = **UGX 400,000.**

**A9.** 9:45 + 1 hr = 10:45, + 50 min = **11:35 am.**

**A10.** One pen = 7,500 ÷ 5 = 1,500. 8 pens = 1,500 × 8 = **UGX 12,000.**

**A11.** Girls = 60% of 50 = 30. Boys = 50 − 30 = **20 boys.**

**A12.** Total = 120,000 + 95,000 + 110,000 + 135,000 + 90,000 = 550,000. Average = 550,000 ÷ 5 = **UGX 110,000.**

**A13.** Increase = 30,000 − 25,000 = 5,000. Percentage = (5,000 ÷ 25,000) × 100 = **20%.**

**A14.** 48,000 ÷ 30 = **UGX 1,600 per day.**

**A15.** Area = length × width = 12 × 8 = **96 m².**

**A16.** Rent + food = 1/4 + 1/3 = 3/12 + 4/12 = 7/12. Left = 1 − 7/12 = **5/12.**

**A17.** 14:20 to 17:05 = 2 hours 45 minutes. (14:20 → 17:20 is 3 hrs, minus 15 min = **2 hr 45 min.**)

**A18.** 40% = 200, so 1% = 5, so 100% = **500.**

**A19.** Total balls = 6 + 4 + 2 = 12. P(blue) = 4/12 = **1/3.**

**A20.** Tax = 30% of 1,500,000 = 450,000. Take-home = 1,500,000 − 450,000 = **UGX 1,050,000.**

**A21.** 3/5 = 60 tasks, so 1/5 = 20 tasks, total 5/5 = **100 tasks.**

**A22.** 4,500 × 2.5 = **UGX 11,250.**

**A23.** Growth = 9,200 − 8,000 = 1,200. Percentage = (1,200 ÷ 8,000) × 100 = **15%.**

**A24.** 12,000 × 7.5 = **UGX 90,000.**

**A25.** 3,800 × 250 = **UGX 950,000.**

# Section 2 — Verbal Reasoning (25 Questions)

Verbal reasoning tests comprehension, vocabulary, and logical use of language. The golden rule: answer **only from the information given**, never from outside knowledge.

For Q1–Q10, choose the word closest in meaning (a synonym).

**Q1.** DILIGENT — (a) lazy (b) hardworking (c) late (d) clever

**Q2.** ABUNDANT — (a) scarce (b) plentiful (c) empty (d) costly

**Q3.** TERMINATE — (a) begin (b) continue (c) end (d) delay

**Q4.** CANDID — (a) dishonest (b) frank (c) shy (d) rude

**Q5.** MITIGATE — (a) worsen (b) reduce (c) ignore (d) cause

For Q6–Q10, choose the word most OPPOSITE in meaning (an antonym).

**Q6.** EXPAND — (a) grow (b) shrink (c) build (d) widen

**Q7.** TRANSPARENT — (a) clear (b) honest (c) opaque (d) open

**Q8.** PERMANENT — (a) lasting (b) temporary (c) fixed (d) stable

**Q9.** SURPLUS — (a) extra (b) shortage (c) profit (d) excess

**Q10.** ASCEND — (a) climb (b) rise (c) descend (d) lift

For Q11–Q15, complete the sentence with the best word.

**Q11.** The manager asked for a ______ report, so keep it short and to the point. (a) lengthy (b) concise (c) vague (d) detailed

**Q12.** Because the evidence was ______, the case was dismissed. (a) strong (b) insufficient (c) clear (d) recent

**Q13.** Her ______ attitude made colleagues trust her quickly. (a) hostile (b) sincere (c) careless (d) secretive

**Q14.** The new policy will ______ from January next year. (a) take effect (b) take place (c) take over (d) take after

**Q15.** Staff must ______ to the company's code of conduct. (a) adhere (b) refer (c) object (d) appeal

For Q16–Q20, read the passage and answer True, False, or Cannot Say.

*Passage: The Ministry announced that all civil servants will receive training in digital records management by the end of the year. The training will be conducted online. Attendance will be compulsory for staff in administrative roles.*

**Q16.** All civil servants must attend the training. (True / False / Cannot Say)

**Q17.** The training will be delivered in person at regional centres. (True / False / Cannot Say)

**Q18.** Administrative staff are required to attend. (True / False / Cannot Say)

**Q19.** The training is free of charge. (True / False / Cannot Say)

**Q20.** The training is scheduled to finish before the year ends. (True / False / Cannot Say)

For Q21–Q25, choose the word that best completes the analogy.

**Q21.** DOCTOR is to HOSPITAL as TEACHER is to ______ (a) student (b) school (c) book (d) lesson

**Q22.** HOT is to COLD as DAY is to ______ (a) sun (b) light (c) night (d) morning

**Q23.** PEN is to WRITE as KNIFE is to ______ (a) sharp (b) cut (c) metal (d) kitchen

**Q24.** SECONDS is to MINUTE as MINUTES is to ______ (a) clock (b) time (c) hour (d) day

**Q25.** AUTHOR is to BOOK as COMPOSER is to ______ (a) piano (b) music (c) singer (d) stage

<!-- pagebreak -->

## Section 2 — Worked Answers

**A1.** (b) hardworking — *diligent* means showing care and effort in work.

**A2.** (b) plentiful — *abundant* means existing in large quantities.

**A3.** (c) end — *terminate* means to bring to an end.

**A4.** (b) frank — *candid* means open and honest in speech.

**A5.** (b) reduce — *mitigate* means to make less severe.

**A6.** (b) shrink — the opposite of expand (to grow larger) is to shrink.

**A7.** (c) opaque — *transparent* (see-through) is opposite to *opaque* (not see-through).

**A8.** (b) temporary — *permanent* (lasting) is opposite to *temporary*.

**A9.** (b) shortage — *surplus* (excess) is opposite to *shortage*.

**A10.** (c) descend — *ascend* (go up) is opposite to *descend* (go down).

**A11.** (b) concise — "short and to the point" signals *concise*.

**A12.** (b) insufficient — a dismissed case implies the evidence was not enough.

**A13.** (b) sincere — only a positive trait builds trust; *sincere* fits.

**A14.** (a) take effect — a policy *takes effect* (begins to apply).

**A15.** (a) adhere — you *adhere to* a code of conduct (follow it).

**A16.** **Cannot Say** — the passage says training is for "all civil servants" for *training* but only says attendance is compulsory for administrative staff. Whether others must attend is not stated.

**A17.** **False** — the passage says the training will be conducted online, not in person.

**A18.** **True** — the passage states attendance is compulsory for staff in administrative roles.

**A19.** **Cannot Say** — cost is never mentioned.

**A20.** **True** — training will happen "by the end of the year," i.e. before the year ends.

**A21.** (b) school — a doctor works in a hospital as a teacher works in a school.

**A22.** (c) night — hot/cold are opposites, as are day/night.

**A23.** (b) cut — a pen is used to write as a knife is used to cut.

**A24.** (c) hour — 60 seconds make a minute as 60 minutes make an hour.

**A25.** (b) music — an author creates a book as a composer creates music.

# Section 3 — Logical & Abstract Reasoning (20 Questions)

Logical reasoning tests pattern-spotting and clear deduction. When stuck, look at what *changes* from one step to the next — that is almost always the rule.

For Q1–Q8, find the next number in the sequence.

**Q1.** 2, 4, 6, 8, ___

**Q2.** 3, 6, 12, 24, ___

**Q3.** 1, 4, 9, 16, ___

**Q4.** 100, 90, 81, 73, ___

**Q5.** 5, 10, 20, 40, ___

**Q6.** 1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, ___

**Q7.** 2, 5, 10, 17, 26, ___

**Q8.** 81, 27, 9, 3, ___

For Q9–Q12, choose the odd one out.

**Q9.** (a) 2 (b) 3 (c) 5 (d) 9

**Q10.** (a) square (b) triangle (c) circle (d) rectangle

**Q11.** (a) 12 (b) 18 (c) 24 (d) 25

**Q12.** (a) dog (b) cat (c) lion (d) sparrow

For Q13–Q16, solve the logical deduction.

**Q13.** All teachers in the school can drive. John is a teacher in the school. Can John drive? (Yes / No / Cannot tell)

**Q14.** Some doctors are runners. All runners are fit. Are all doctors fit? (Yes / No / Cannot tell)

**Q15.** If it rains, the match is cancelled. The match was NOT cancelled. Did it rain? (Yes / No / Cannot tell)

**Q16.** Mary is taller than Sara. Sara is taller than Jane. Who is the shortest?

For Q17–Q20, complete the letter pattern.

**Q17.** A, C, E, G, ___

**Q18.** Z, X, V, T, ___

**Q19.** AB, CD, EF, GH, ___

**Q20.** A, B, D, G, K, ___

## Section 3 — Worked Answers

**A1.** **10** — add 2 each time.

**A2.** **48** — each term doubles (×2).

**A3.** **25** — square numbers: 1², 2², 3², 4², **5²**.

**A4.** **66** — subtract 10, then 9, then 8, then 7: 73 − 7 = 66.

**A5.** **80** — each term doubles.

**A6.** **13** — Fibonacci: each term is the sum of the two before (5 + 8 = 13).

**A7.** **37** — differences are 3, 5, 7, 9, then 11: 26 + 11 = 37. (Also: n² + 1.)

**A8.** **1** — divide by 3 each time: 3 ÷ 3 = 1.

**A9.** (d) 9 — 2, 3, 5 are prime numbers; 9 is not (3 × 3).

**A10.** (c) circle — square, triangle, rectangle have straight sides; a circle has none.

**A11.** (d) 25 — 12, 18, 24 are multiples of 6; 25 is not.

**A12.** (d) sparrow — dog, cat, lion are mammals; a sparrow is a bird.

**A13.** **Yes** — all teachers can drive; John is a teacher, so John can drive.

**A14.** **Cannot tell** — only *some* doctors are runners. Doctors who are not runners may or may not be fit.

**A15.** **No** — if rain always cancels the match, and the match was not cancelled, then it did not rain.

**A16.** **Jane** — Mary > Sara > Jane, so Jane is shortest.

**A17.** **I** — skip one letter each time (A, _, C, _, E, _, G, _, I).

**A18.** **R** — going backwards, skip one letter each time (Z, Y, X, W, V, U, T, S, R).

**A19.** **IJ** — consecutive letter pairs: GH then IJ.

**A20.** **P** — gaps increase by 1: +1, +2, +3, +4, then +5: K(11) + 5 = P(16).

# Section 4 — Situational Judgement (20 Questions)

Situational judgement tests how you behave at work. There is usually a "most appropriate" and a "least appropriate" action. The safe instinct: choose the answer that is **professional, honest, follows proper procedure, and protects the organisation and the public** — never the fastest or most personally convenient.

For each scenario, choose the MOST appropriate action.

**Q1.** You notice a colleague has made an error in a report that is about to be sent to a client. You should:
(a) Say nothing — it's not your report. (b) Quietly tell the colleague so they can correct it. (c) Report them to the manager immediately. (d) Fix it yourself without telling anyone.

**Q2.** A customer is angry and shouting at you about a problem that is not your fault. You should:
(a) Shout back to defend yourself. (b) Stay calm, listen, and try to help or find someone who can. (c) Walk away. (d) Tell them it's not your problem.

**Q3.** You are given a task with a deadline you know you cannot meet. You should:
(a) Say nothing and miss the deadline. (b) Inform your supervisor early and discuss options. (c) Rush and submit poor work on time. (d) Ask a colleague to do it secretly.

**Q4.** You see a coworker taking office supplies for personal use. You should:
(a) Take some yourself too. (b) Ignore it. (c) Follow the organisation's reporting procedure. (d) Confront them aggressively in front of others.

**Q5.** You don't understand an instruction your manager gave you. You should:
(a) Guess and hope for the best. (b) Politely ask the manager to clarify. (c) Do nothing until asked again. (d) Ask a junior colleague instead.

**Q6.** A friend asks you to share confidential information from work. You should:
(a) Share it — they're a friend. (b) Refuse politely and keep it confidential. (c) Share only a little. (d) Ask your manager if you can share it with friends.

**Q7.** You finish your tasks early and have spare time at work. You should:
(a) Browse social media. (b) Leave early without telling anyone. (c) Ask if you can help others or take on more work. (d) Pretend to be busy.

**Q8.** Two team members are in a heated disagreement. As a colleague, you should:
(a) Take one person's side. (b) Encourage them to discuss calmly or involve a supervisor if needed. (c) Ignore it completely. (d) Spread the story to others.

**Q9.** You realise you made a mistake that no one has noticed yet. You should:
(a) Hide it and hope no one finds out. (b) Report it and propose how to fix it. (c) Blame someone else. (d) Wait to see if it causes a problem.

**Q10.** A supervisor asks you to do something you believe is against company policy. You should:
(a) Refuse rudely. (b) Do it without question. (c) Respectfully raise your concern and seek clarification through proper channels. (d) Tell other staff the supervisor is corrupt.

**Q11.** You are running late for an important meeting due to transport. You should:
(a) Say nothing and arrive late. (b) Call ahead to inform them as soon as you know. (c) Skip the meeting entirely. (d) Make up an excuse later.

**Q12.** A client offers you a personal gift to speed up their application. You should:
(a) Accept it quietly. (b) Decline politely and follow the standard process for everyone. (c) Accept and do the favour. (d) Ask for more.

**Q13.** You disagree with a decision made in a team meeting. You should:
(a) Complain to colleagues afterwards. (b) Raise your view respectfully during the meeting or with your manager. (c) Refuse to follow the decision. (d) Stay silent and resentful.

**Q14.** A new colleague is struggling and asks for help, but you are busy. You should:
(a) Ignore them. (b) Help briefly now or arrange a clear time to assist soon. (c) Tell them to figure it out alone. (d) Report them for being slow.

**Q15.** You notice a safety hazard in the workplace. You should:
(a) Step around it and continue. (b) Report it through the proper channel promptly. (c) Wait for someone else to notice. (d) Fix it yourself even if untrained and unsafe.

**Q16.** You are asked to work on a task outside your usual duties. You should:
(a) Refuse — it's not your job. (b) Attempt it willingly and ask for guidance if needed. (c) Do it badly to avoid being asked again. (d) Complain to everyone.

**Q17.** You receive credit for work a colleague mostly did. You should:
(a) Accept the credit silently. (b) Acknowledge your colleague's contribution honestly. (c) Deny they helped. (d) Offer to split future credit only.

**Q18.** A deadline forces a choice between speed and accuracy on important figures. You should:
(a) Submit fast with possible errors. (b) Communicate the trade-off to your supervisor and prioritise accuracy on critical figures. (c) Guess the figures. (d) Submit late with no warning.

**Q19.** You overhear confidential information by accident. You should:
(a) Share it with the team. (b) Keep it confidential and not act on it. (c) Use it to your advantage. (d) Confront the people involved.

**Q20.** Your workload is consistently too high to manage well. You should:
(a) Burn out silently. (b) Discuss it honestly with your manager to find a solution. (c) Quietly drop tasks without telling anyone. (d) Blame your colleagues.

<!-- pagebreak -->

## Section 4 — Worked Answers

The most appropriate answer is **(b)** for every question in this section. Here is the principle behind each:

**A1.** (b) — Correcting errors protects the client and your colleague; quiet, direct help is professional.

**A2.** (b) — Staying calm and helping de-escalates and serves the customer.

**A3.** (b) — Early, honest communication lets the team plan; hiding problems makes them worse.

**A4.** (c) — Following proper procedure is correct; theft must be reported, not copied or ignored.

**A5.** (b) — Asking for clarification prevents costly mistakes and shows responsibility.

**A6.** (b) — Confidentiality is a duty; friendship does not override it.

**A7.** (c) — Offering to help shows initiative and value to the team.

**A8.** (b) — Encouraging calm discussion, escalating if needed, keeps the team functional.

**A9.** (b) — Owning a mistake and proposing a fix builds trust; hiding it destroys it.

**A10.** (c) — Raise concerns respectfully through proper channels — neither blind obedience nor public accusation.

**A11.** (b) — Informing people early is courteous and professional.

**A12.** (b) — Declining gifts and treating everyone equally protects integrity.

**A13.** (b) — Voice disagreement respectfully and in the right forum, then commit to the decision.

**A14.** (b) — Helping a colleague, even briefly or later, supports the team without abandoning your own work.

**A15.** (b) — Promptly reporting hazards protects everyone; don't ignore or act unsafely.

**A16.** (b) — Willingness to help and ask for guidance shows a strong work attitude.

**A17.** (b) — Honest acknowledgement of others' work is fair and builds trust.

**A18.** (b) — Communicating trade-offs and protecting accuracy on critical work is responsible.

**A19.** (b) — Confidential information stays confidential, even if heard by accident.

**A20.** (b) — Raising workload issues honestly leads to real solutions; silence or blame does not.
