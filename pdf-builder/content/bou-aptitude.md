# How to Use This Pack

Bank of Uganda runs one of the most competitive recruitment processes in the country. Most technical and graduate positions require candidates to sit a written aptitude and knowledge assessment before the interview stage — covering **numerical reasoning, verbal ability, and subject-specific content** relevant to finance, economics, and banking.

This pack is built specifically for that test. It has **80 real-style practice questions** across the four areas BOU assesses, with a **full worked answer for every question**. The numerical questions are finance-flavoured. The knowledge section covers the central-banking and economics fundamentals BOU expects its candidates to know.

::: tip How to prepare for BOU
Do the reasoning sections under a timer (about 60–75 seconds per question). For the Banking & Economics Knowledge section, read every explanation carefully — these are the facts a BOU panel expects you to know in the interview too, so this section does double duty. Drill your weakest area twice.
:::

::: note A note on accuracy
This pack reflects widely-published, general knowledge about central banking and the Ugandan economy. Always cross-check current figures (such as the latest Central Bank Rate or inflation rate) on the official Bank of Uganda website before your test, as these change over time.
:::

Answers for each section are grouped at the **end of that section** so you can test yourself first.

# Section 1 — Numerical Reasoning (Finance-Focused) — 20 Questions

BOU's numerical questions lean toward money, interest, inflation, and financial data. You do not need advanced maths — just accuracy with percentages, ratios, and rates.

**Q1.** A deposit of UGX 5,000,000 earns simple interest at 8% per year. How much interest after 1 year?

**Q2.** Inflation is 6% per year. An item costing UGX 50,000 today will cost how much in a year (assuming it rises with inflation)?

**Q3.** The exchange rate is UGX 3,800 per USD. How many US dollars can you buy with UGX 1,900,000?

**Q4.** A bank charges 2% on a transaction of UGX 4,500,000. What is the fee?

**Q5.** A loan of UGX 10,000,000 is repaid over 10 equal monthly instalments (principal only). What is each instalment?

**Q6.** A company's revenue rose from UGX 80m to UGX 100m. What is the percentage increase?

**Q7.** If the Central Bank Rate is 10% and a bank lends at 4% above it, what is the lending rate?

**Q8.** A portfolio of UGX 20,000,000 loses 15% of its value. What is it now worth?

**Q9.** A salary of UGX 2,400,000 is paid monthly. What is the annual salary?

**Q10.** 30% of a bank's 2,000 customers use mobile banking. How many is that?

**Q11.** A bond pays UGX 90,000 interest on a value of UGX 1,000,000. What is the interest rate?

**Q12.** A trader converts USD 500 at UGX 3,800 per dollar. How many shillings does she get?

**Q13.** Costs are UGX 12m and revenue is UGX 15m. What is the profit margin as a percentage of revenue?

**Q14.** A sum doubles at 10% simple interest per year. How many years does it take?

**Q15.** Quarterly profits were 5m, 7m, 6m, 10m. What was the average quarterly profit?

**Q16.** A fee of UGX 25,000 includes 18% VAT. Approximately how much is the VAT portion? (to nearest 100)

**Q17.** A budget of UGX 60m allocates 1/4 to salaries and 1/3 to operations. How much is left?

**Q18.** If GDP grows 5% from a base of UGX 200 trillion, what is the new GDP?

**Q19.** A reserve requirement is 8% of UGX 50,000,000,000 in deposits. How much must be held?

**Q20.** An account grows from UGX 750,000 to UGX 900,000. What is the percentage growth?

<!-- pagebreak -->

## Section 1 — Worked Answers

**A1.** 8% of 5,000,000 = **UGX 400,000.**

**A2.** 6% of 50,000 = 3,000. New price = 50,000 + 3,000 = **UGX 53,000.**

**A3.** 1,900,000 ÷ 3,800 = **USD 500.**

**A4.** 2% of 4,500,000 = **UGX 90,000.**

**A5.** 10,000,000 ÷ 10 = **UGX 1,000,000 per instalment.**

**A6.** Increase = 100 − 80 = 20m. (20 ÷ 80) × 100 = **25%.**

**A7.** 10% + 4% = **14%.**

**A8.** 15% of 20,000,000 = 3,000,000. New value = 20m − 3m = **UGX 17,000,000.**

**A9.** 2,400,000 × 12 = **UGX 28,800,000.**

**A10.** 30% of 2,000 = **600 customers.**

**A11.** (90,000 ÷ 1,000,000) × 100 = **9%.**

**A12.** 500 × 3,800 = **UGX 1,900,000.**

**A13.** Profit = 15m − 12m = 3m. Margin = (3 ÷ 15) × 100 = **20%.**

**A14.** At 10% simple interest, you earn 10% of the original each year. To gain 100% (double) = 100 ÷ 10 = **10 years.**

**A15.** (5 + 7 + 6 + 10) ÷ 4 = 28 ÷ 4 = **UGX 7,000,000.**

**A16.** VAT portion = 25,000 × (18 ÷ 118) = 25,000 × 0.1525 ≈ **UGX 3,800.**

**A17.** Salaries = 1/4 = 15m. Operations = 1/3 = 20m. Used = 35m. Left = 60m − 35m = **UGX 25,000,000.**

**A18.** 5% of 200 trillion = 10 trillion. New GDP = **UGX 210 trillion.**

**A19.** 8% of 50,000,000,000 = **UGX 4,000,000,000.**

**A20.** Growth = 900,000 − 750,000 = 150,000. (150,000 ÷ 750,000) × 100 = **20%.**

# Section 2 — Verbal Reasoning — 15 Questions

Answer only from the information given. Read carefully.

For Q1–Q5, choose the word closest in meaning.

**Q1.** PRUDENT — (a) reckless (b) careful (c) generous (d) wealthy

**Q2.** LIQUIDITY — (a) profit (b) availability of cash (c) debt (d) tax

**Q3.** VOLATILE — (a) stable (b) unpredictable (c) cheap (d) rising

**Q4.** DEFICIT — (a) surplus (b) shortfall (c) balance (d) profit

**Q5.** REGULATE — (a) ignore (b) control (c) abolish (d) fund

For Q6–Q10, read the passage and answer True, False, or Cannot Say.

*Passage: A central bank's main objective is to maintain price stability by managing inflation. It uses tools such as the policy interest rate to influence the cost of borrowing. When inflation is high, the central bank may raise interest rates to reduce spending. The central bank also supervises commercial banks to protect depositors.*

**Q6.** A central bank aims to keep prices stable. (True / False / Cannot Say)

**Q7.** Raising interest rates is used to increase spending. (True / False / Cannot Say)

**Q8.** The central bank supervises commercial banks. (True / False / Cannot Say)

**Q9.** The central bank sets the salaries of commercial bank staff. (True / False / Cannot Say)

**Q10.** Interest rates are one tool the central bank uses. (True / False / Cannot Say)

For Q11–Q15, complete the sentence with the best word.

**Q11.** The bank kept a large cash ______ to meet sudden withdrawals. (a) reserve (b) loan (c) debt (d) loss

**Q12.** High inflation ______ the purchasing power of money. (a) increases (b) erodes (c) fixes (d) doubles

**Q13.** Auditors check accounts to ensure they are ______. (a) profitable (b) accurate (c) large (d) secret

**Q14.** A ______ budget spends exactly what it earns. (a) deficit (b) surplus (c) balanced (d) flexible

**Q15.** Diversifying investments helps to ______ risk. (a) increase (b) reduce (c) ignore (d) create

<!-- pagebreak -->

## Section 2 — Worked Answers

**A1.** (b) careful — *prudent* means showing care and good judgement, especially with money.

**A2.** (b) availability of cash — *liquidity* is how easily assets can be turned into cash.

**A3.** (b) unpredictable — *volatile* means likely to change rapidly and unpredictably.

**A4.** (b) shortfall — a *deficit* is when spending exceeds income.

**A5.** (b) control — to *regulate* is to control or supervise by rules.

**A6.** **True** — the passage states price stability is the main objective.

**A7.** **False** — raising rates is used to *reduce* spending, not increase it.

**A8.** **True** — the passage says it supervises commercial banks.

**A9.** **Cannot Say** — staff salaries are never mentioned.

**A10.** **True** — the policy interest rate is named as a tool.

**A11.** (a) reserve — a cash *reserve* meets sudden withdrawals.

**A12.** (b) erodes — inflation *erodes* (reduces) purchasing power.

**A13.** (b) accurate — audits check accounts are *accurate*.

**A14.** (c) balanced — a *balanced* budget spends exactly what it earns.

**A15.** (b) reduce — diversifying *reduces* risk.

# Section 3 — Banking & Economics Knowledge — 25 Questions

This is the subject-specific section BOU values most. These are facts a strong candidate should know. Cross-check current figures on the official Bank of Uganda website before your test.

**Q1.** What is the primary role of a central bank like Bank of Uganda?
(a) To make profit for shareholders (b) To maintain price stability and a sound financial system (c) To give loans to individuals (d) To collect taxes

**Q2.** What does "inflation" mean?
(a) A fall in prices (b) A general rise in prices over time (c) An increase in employment (d) A rise in exports

**Q3.** The interest rate set by the central bank to guide the economy is called the:
(a) Exchange rate (b) Central Bank Rate (CBR) (c) VAT rate (d) Income tax rate

**Q4.** Which institution issues Uganda's currency, the shilling?
(a) Ministry of Finance (b) Bank of Uganda (c) URA (d) Commercial banks

**Q5.** What is "monetary policy"?
(a) Government spending and taxation (b) Central bank actions to manage money supply and interest rates (c) Trade agreements (d) Company budgets

**Q6.** What is "fiscal policy"?
(a) Central bank interest rate decisions (b) Government decisions on spending and taxation (c) Bank lending rules (d) Stock market rules

**Q7.** When the central bank RAISES interest rates, borrowing generally becomes:
(a) Cheaper (b) More expensive (c) Unchanged (d) Free

**Q8.** "GDP" stands for:
(a) Gross Domestic Product (b) General Domestic Profit (c) Government Debt Plan (d) Gross Deposit Percentage

**Q9.** A "reserve requirement" is:
(a) Money banks must keep and not lend out (b) A type of loan (c) A tax on profits (d) An exchange rate

**Q10.** What does "liquidity" refer to in banking?
(a) Total profit (b) How easily assets can be converted to cash (c) Number of branches (d) Staff numbers

**Q11.** Which of these is a function of commercial banks (not the central bank)?
(a) Issuing currency (b) Accepting deposits and lending to the public (c) Setting the Central Bank Rate (d) Supervising other banks

**Q12.** "Forex" is short for:
(a) Forward export (b) Foreign exchange (c) Formal extension (d) Forecast index

**Q13.** If the shilling WEAKENS against the dollar, imports generally become:
(a) Cheaper (b) More expensive (c) Free (d) Unchanged

**Q14.** A "budget deficit" occurs when:
(a) Revenue exceeds spending (b) Spending exceeds revenue (c) The budget is balanced (d) There is no budget

**Q15.** Which body is responsible for collecting taxes in Uganda?
(a) Bank of Uganda (b) Uganda Revenue Authority (URA) (c) NSSF (d) UBOS

**Q16.** "Depreciation" of a currency means its value has:
(a) Risen (b) Fallen (c) Stayed the same (d) Been fixed

**Q17.** The main goal of raising interest rates during high inflation is to:
(a) Encourage borrowing (b) Reduce spending and cool inflation (c) Increase imports (d) Lower the exchange rate

**Q18.** A "treasury bond" is:
(a) A short-term company share (b) A long-term government debt instrument (c) A type of insurance (d) A savings account

**Q19.** "Financial inclusion" means:
(a) Excluding poor customers (b) Giving more people access to financial services (c) Closing rural branches (d) Raising bank fees

**Q20.** Which measures the cost of living changes over time?
(a) GDP (b) Consumer Price Index (CPI) (c) Exchange rate (d) Reserve ratio

**Q21.** A bank's "non-performing loans" are loans that:
(a) Are fully repaid (b) Borrowers have failed to repay on time (c) Earn high interest (d) Are given to staff

**Q22.** The interbank market is where:
(a) Banks lend to each other (b) Banks sell shares (c) Customers deposit money (d) Taxes are paid

**Q23.** "Devaluation" differs from depreciation because it is:
(a) A market-driven fall (b) A deliberate official reduction in currency value (c) An increase in value (d) A rise in inflation

**Q24.** Which is a sign of a HEALTHY economy?
(a) Very high inflation (b) Stable prices and steady growth (c) Rapidly falling currency (d) Rising unemployment

**Q25.** The "money supply" refers to:
(a) The total amount of money circulating in the economy (b) A bank's profit (c) Government tax revenue (d) The number of banks

<!-- pagebreak -->

## Section 3 — Worked Answers

**A1.** (b) — A central bank's core role is price stability and a sound financial system, not profit.

**A2.** (b) — Inflation is a general, sustained rise in prices over time.

**A3.** (b) — The Central Bank Rate (CBR) is the key policy rate.

**A4.** (b) — Bank of Uganda issues the national currency.

**A5.** (b) — Monetary policy is the central bank's management of money supply and interest rates.

**A6.** (b) — Fiscal policy is government taxation and spending (set by the Ministry of Finance).

**A7.** (b) — Higher rates make borrowing more expensive.

**A8.** (a) — Gross Domestic Product, the total value of goods and services produced.

**A9.** (a) — Reserve requirements are funds banks must hold rather than lend.

**A10.** (b) — Liquidity is how quickly assets become cash.

**A11.** (b) — Accepting deposits and lending is a commercial bank function; the rest belong to the central bank.

**A12.** (b) — Forex = foreign exchange.

**A13.** (b) — A weaker shilling means imports cost more in local currency.

**A14.** (b) — A deficit means spending exceeds revenue.

**A15.** (b) — URA collects taxes.

**A16.** (b) — Depreciation means the currency's value has fallen.

**A17.** (b) — Raising rates cools inflation by reducing spending.

**A18.** (b) — A treasury bond is long-term government borrowing.

**A19.** (b) — Financial inclusion expands access to financial services.

**A20.** (b) — The Consumer Price Index (CPI) tracks the cost of living.

**A21.** (b) — Non-performing loans are not being repaid on time.

**A22.** (a) — The interbank market is where banks lend to one another.

**A23.** (b) — Devaluation is a deliberate official reduction; depreciation is market-driven.

**A24.** (b) — Stable prices and steady growth signal a healthy economy.

**A25.** (a) — Money supply is the total money circulating in the economy.

# Section 4 — Situational Judgement — 20 Questions

For each scenario, choose the MOST appropriate action. The safe instinct in a banking environment: be professional, honest, follow procedure, protect confidentiality, and protect the institution and the public.

**Q1.** You spot a discrepancy in a financial report before it goes to senior management. You should:
(a) Ignore it. (b) Verify it and promptly inform your supervisor. (c) Quietly change the figure yourself. (d) Send the report anyway.

**Q2.** A customer asks you to process a transaction that seems suspicious. You should:
(a) Process it quickly. (b) Follow the bank's procedure and escalate to the relevant unit. (c) Refuse rudely. (d) Ask the customer for a tip.

**Q3.** A colleague asks you to share a customer's account details casually. You should:
(a) Share them. (b) Decline and uphold customer confidentiality. (c) Share only the balance. (d) Ask the customer first.

**Q4.** You are unsure how to handle a complex compliance question. You should:
(a) Guess. (b) Consult the relevant policy or a senior colleague. (c) Ignore it. (d) Tell the customer it's impossible.

**Q5.** You notice a process that wastes time and could be improved. You should:
(a) Say nothing. (b) Propose the improvement through the proper channel. (c) Change it secretly. (d) Complain to customers.

**Q6.** You make an error that affects a customer's balance. You should:
(a) Hope no one notices. (b) Report it immediately and follow correction procedure. (c) Blame the system. (d) Adjust it quietly without record.

**Q7.** A friend asks for insider information about the bank's plans. You should:
(a) Tell them. (b) Refuse and keep it confidential. (c) Hint at it. (d) Ask a manager if it's okay to share.

**Q8.** During a busy period, a customer is frustrated by waiting. You should:
(a) Tell them to leave. (b) Stay courteous, acknowledge the wait, and serve efficiently. (c) Ignore them. (d) Argue.

**Q9.** You are offered a gift by a client to prioritise their loan. You should:
(a) Accept it. (b) Decline politely and follow standard procedure for all clients. (c) Accept and help them. (d) Ask for cash instead.

**Q10.** You disagree with a new procedure from management. You should:
(a) Refuse to follow it. (b) Raise your concern through proper channels, then comply. (c) Ignore it. (d) Complain publicly.

**Q11.** You realise a colleague is breaching a compliance rule. You should:
(a) Join in. (b) Follow the bank's reporting procedure. (c) Ignore it. (d) Confront them loudly.

**Q12.** A deadline pressures you to skip a verification step. You should:
(a) Skip it to save time. (b) Complete the verification — accuracy and compliance come first. (c) Guess the result. (d) Ask a junior to skip it.

**Q13.** You are assigned work outside your usual role. You should:
(a) Refuse. (b) Take it on willingly and seek guidance where needed. (c) Do it poorly. (d) Complain.

**Q14.** A customer doesn't understand a financial product. You should:
(a) Rush them to sign. (b) Explain clearly and honestly so they can decide. (c) Tell them to ask elsewhere. (d) Exaggerate the benefits.

**Q15.** You find sensitive documents left unattended. You should:
(a) Read them. (b) Secure them and report per procedure. (c) Leave them. (d) Take a photo.

**Q16.** A senior colleague asks you to bend a rule "just this once." You should:
(a) Do it to please them. (b) Respectfully decline and follow the correct procedure. (c) Do it and stay silent. (d) Report them angrily to everyone.

**Q17.** You receive praise for a team's work. You should:
(a) Take all credit. (b) Acknowledge the team's contribution. (c) Deny others helped. (d) Stay silent.

**Q18.** You are consistently overloaded and quality is slipping. You should:
(a) Keep quiet and burn out. (b) Raise it honestly with your manager to find a solution. (c) Drop tasks silently. (d) Blame colleagues.

**Q19.** A customer becomes abusive. You should:
(a) Abuse them back. (b) Stay calm, follow the bank's procedure for difficult customers, and seek help if needed. (c) Walk away. (d) Threaten them.

**Q20.** You overhear confidential market-sensitive information by accident. You should:
(a) Trade on it. (b) Keep it confidential and do not act on it. (c) Share with friends. (d) Post it online.

<!-- pagebreak -->

## Section 4 — Worked Answers

The most appropriate answer is **(b)** for every question. The principle behind each:

**A1.** Verify and report discrepancies — accuracy protects the institution.

**A2.** Follow procedure and escalate suspicious transactions — compliance is mandatory in banking.

**A3.** Customer confidentiality is a legal and ethical duty.

**A4.** Consult policy or a senior on compliance questions rather than guessing.

**A5.** Propose improvements through proper channels.

**A6.** Report errors immediately and follow correction procedure.

**A7.** Insider information stays confidential, even from friends.

**A8.** Courtesy and efficiency under pressure protect the customer relationship.

**A9.** Decline gifts; treat all clients equally to protect integrity.

**A10.** Voice disagreement properly, then comply (disagree and commit).

**A11.** Report compliance breaches through the proper procedure.

**A12.** Never skip verification — accuracy and compliance come before speed.

**A13.** Willingness to take on new work, seeking guidance, shows a strong attitude.

**A14.** Explain products honestly so customers make informed choices.

**A15.** Secure and report unattended sensitive documents.

**A16.** Decline to bend rules, even for seniors — follow correct procedure.

**A17.** Acknowledge the team honestly.

**A18.** Raise workload issues honestly to find real solutions.

**A19.** Stay calm and follow procedure with difficult customers.

**A20.** Confidential, market-sensitive information must never be acted on or shared.
